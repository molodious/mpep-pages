What You'll Learn

Focused, practical modules that equip you to handle critical systems with confidence.

UPS Systems +

Uninterruptible power supply system design, battery maintenance, failure modes, and how to ensure reliable power continuity in high-stakes environments.

Cooling +

CRAHs, CRACs, airflow management, and design strategies for redundancy and resilience in critical cooling infrastructure.

Fire Protection +

Detection systems, suppression methods, and common missteps in code compliance for mission-critical facilities.

Static Transfer Switches & PDUs +

How power is safely distributed and switched across redundant sources — the mechanics and the decision-making that keeps systems online.

Capacity Management +

Balancing power, cooling, space, and network resources to avoid the twin failure modes of under-provisioning and over-provisioning.

Maintenance Management +

Proactive preventive maintenance systems designed for operational longevity and maximum uptime in critical environments.

Monitoring & Controls +

BMS, EPMS, and alarm configuration — keeping critical infrastructure visible, responsive, and under control at all times.

Incident Management +

Responding quickly and effectively to outages, with a focus on safety, communication, and minimizing downtime.

Energy Efficiency +

Finding real opportunities to reduce power usage without compromising system reliability or uptime commitments.

Commissioning +

How to execute, test, and validate systems so everything works — on Day 1 and Day 1,000.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this course accepted for PDH credit in my state?

In most states, yes. This course meets continuing education standards for licensed PEs and includes a certificate of completion. Most states accept self-directed learning if it's relevant and well-documented. A few states (NY, FL, NC, LA, IN, NJ) have more specific requirements — check with your board if you're licensed in one of them.

How many PDH hours will I earn?

12 PDH hours upon successful completion of the course and final exam.

Is there a test I need to pass?

Yes. There's a short quiz at the end of each section and a final exam at the end of the course. A 70% passing score is required to earn your certificate. You can review material and retake quizzes if needed.

Will I receive a certificate?

Yes. Once you complete the course and pass the final exam, you'll receive a certificate confirming your 12 PDH credits.

Can I access this on mobile?

Yes. Watch on any device with internet access. Progress is tracked automatically.

Do I need a PE license to enroll?

No. The course is designed for licensed PEs seeking PDH credit, but anyone interested in critical systems engineering is welcome to enroll.
